Publications

Exploring the Effectiveness of Large Language Models in Financial Question Answering (Undergraduate Thesis)

Accepted on the 3rd International Conference on Computing Advancements (ICCA 2024)

This study examines the effectiveness of two advanced LLMs: Llama2 by Meta and Gemma by Google in Financial Question-Answering tasks. Using Zero-Shot, Few-Shot Prompt Engineering, and Supervised Fine-Tuning, the study finds that while Llama2 generates responses more frequently, it tends to hallucinate whereas Gemma struggles to deliver accurate answers. These results underscore the need for further refinement of LLMs for specialized financial applications.

Authors: Mondol Mridul Provakar, Emrana Kabir Hashi

GitHub
Evaluating the Text Summarization Efficiency of Large Language Models

Accepted on the 2nd International Conference on Information and Communication Technology (ICICT 2024)

This study evaluates Llama-2 and Gemma for text summarization using metrics like BLEU, ROUGE and BERTScore. Llama-2, especially after fine-tuning, outperforms Gemma producing more contextually accurate summaries. Enhancing LLMs for summarization demands improved model design, advanced fine-tuning methods and diverse training data to strengthen domain adaptability.

Author: Mondol Mridul Provakar
